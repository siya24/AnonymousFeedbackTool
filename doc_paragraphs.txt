Introduction
Legal Aid South Africa is committed to fostering an inclusive, ethical, and human-centred work environment. However, to ensure that this vision is fully realized, it is essential to provide all employees with a safe, confidential, and accessible platform to raise concerns related to unfair treatment, discrimination, harassment, or psychologically unsafe working conditions.
This initiative empowers employees to communicate openly and contribute to a culture of psychological safety, transparency, and organizational accountability.
Purpose of Document
This document outlines a comprehensive overview of the functional, technical and security requirements for the Anonymous Feedback Tool. It is intended for all stakeholders involved in a design, development, implementation of the tool, including:
Software Developer
HR and Ethics Officers
Quality Assurance Team

Business Problem
Legal Aid South Africa currently does not have an anonymous, mechanism for employees to report non-humanistic issues without fear of retaliation, bias and exposure.  This gap reduces the likelihood of early intervention and weakens the organizational culture of openness.
Solution Objectives
The Anonymous Feedback Tool will support the following goals:
Psychological Safety: Enables honest and open communication without fear.
Early Intervention: Helps management identify toxic behaviours or systemic issues early.
Improved Culture: Strengthens Legal Aid South Africa’s values of integrity, respect, and service excellence.
Compliance: Supports Employment Equity, Occupational Health and Safety, and Labour Relations best practices.
Accessibility: Enable access from any location, at any time, via the public facing Legal Aid South Africa website
Scope of Requirements
This section of the document lists all the business requirements that have been identified for the solution. 
Anonymous Reporting Form (Legal Aid South Africa Website)
The Anonymous Reporting Form should be accessible via the link on the Legal Aid South Africa Website
No identifiable information should be collected, such as names, email addresses, or employee Numbers.
 Immediately after accessing the Anonymous Reporting form, generate the Support and Protection message to remind the Employees of the available psychosocial and HR support and reassured that retaliation or victimisation is strictly prohibited and will attract disciplinary action. (HR to provide the message)
Employees should have an option to indicate if the Feeback captured is New or an Update.
If the Feeback is New
Employees must categorize their feedback by selecting from the following list below:
Discrimination
Harassment or Bullying
Unfair Workload Distribution
Managerial Misconduct
Psychological Safety Concerns
Other (with text field)
A Free Text Field (e.g., 5000-character limit) is required for a detailed description.
An option to upload files should be available (e.g. screenshots/documents/ photos and audio files).
When submitting the feedback, this application must generate a reference number, and a warning message advising the employee to keep the reference number safe, as it will be required:
to track the matter on the Employee Reporting link that will be available on the   Internal HR Self-Service or Portal
as the same reference number will be used when providing an Update or requested information. 
If the Feedback is an Update
Employee must capture the Reference no
The system must return the previously captured information
The system must generate a free text field (e.g., 5000-character limit) for a detailed information.
An option to upload files should be available (e.g. screenshots/documents/ photos and audio files).
When submitting the feedback, this application must generate a reference number, and a confirmation message advising the employee:
to track the matter on the Employee Reporting link that will be available on the Internal HR Self-Service or Portal
HR Management Console (Intranet – Restricted Access)
System must automatically generate an entry on the HR Management Console after the anonymous feedback is submitted.
Access should be restricted to authorized HR and Ethics Officers only
System should capture date and time of submission.
System should immediately send automated email notification of submission to the authorized HR Officer 
If the submission is not acknowledged after 48 hours, send another email notification of submission to the authorized HR Officer 
If the submission is not acknowledged after 72 hours, send the escalation to the Ethics Officer
The authorised HR officer on the HR Management console should be able to:
View and acknowledge all feedback entries, immediately after submission. 
As part of acknowledgement of submission capture priority and the stage of investigation.
Filter/sort entries by category, date, and status and reference number.
Prioritize cases by frequency, severity, and potential impact.
Capture the anonymized summary of the submission, and this summary will be visible on the Employee Report link.
To capture action taken and progress
Update the investigation status by selecting the options below:
Investigation pending
Investigation in progress 
Investigation completed
Capture optional comments when updating the investigation status.
 When the investigation completed status is selected, a mandatory Outcome Reached field should be presented to capture detailed outcome comments.
Optional internal notes/comments section for authorized users.
The system should keep a comprehensive audit trail of all activities (e.g. logged incident, update, etc.)
Dashboard with trend visualization (e.g., cases per category per quarter).

Employee Reporting Link (Internal HR Self-Service or Portal)
Create a link for the employees on the HR portal to view a Report of anonymized summaries of cases.
The report should display the following fields:
Date Logged
Category
Current Investigation Status
Outcome Reached, and the comments captured
The report should include a search functionality using the Category, Date, and Investigation status and reference number.
The report should refresh every time the HR console is updated.
No personal identifiable or sensitive internal information should be visible.
.
Security Requirements:
All data must be encrypted in transit and at rest.
Anonymous reports and management logs must be stored in a secure environment, accessible only to authorized personnel.
Implement role-based access control (RBAC) on the Management Console.
Regular security audits, penetration tests, and access log reviews must be scheduled.
Ensure compliance with the Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA).
Include file scanning for uploaded documents to prevent malware.
Technical Requirements:
Compatible across all major browsers (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari).
Mobile and desktop responsive design.
Audit logs should be retained for a minimum of 5 years.
